    Despite reaping profits of more than $340m since 2013, holding company has paid little tax

A key company in the business empire of Australia’s richest man, has paid very little tax since 2013 despite reaping profits totalling more than $340m over the same period, corporate and tax records show.

Mr. P., who is ranked No 1 on rich lists maintained by the Australian Financial Review and The Australian, runs a globe-spanning packaging business and has raised money for both sides of politics.

At the opening factory in the US last month described Mr. P. as the “Don Bradman of job creation”. Earlier this year, before the federal election, he hosted a $5,000-a-head fundraiser for then opposition leader Bill Shorten at his family’s historic Melbourne mansion, Raheen.

His Consolidated Holdings, which is ultimately owned by family trust, collects dividends from other companies in the group and passes them on to the trust.
